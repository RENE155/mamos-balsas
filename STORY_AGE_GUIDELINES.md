# Bedtime Story Guidelines by Age Group

Research-based best practices for creating bedtime stories in the MamosBalsas app.

## Quick Reference Table

| Age Group | Duration | Word Count | Paragraphs | Sentences/Para | Key Features |
|-----------|----------|------------|------------|----------------|--------------|
| 0-2 years | 2-4 min | 50-150 | 1-3 | 1-2 | Sounds, rhythm, repetition |
| 2-4 years | 4-6 min | 150-300 | 3-5 | 2-3 | Simple plot, familiar objects |
| 4-6 years | 6-10 min | 300-500 | 5-7 | 3-4 | Mini-adventures, emotions |
| 6-8 years | 10-15 min | 500-800 | 7-9 | 4-5 | Complex plots, lessons |
| 8-9 years | 15-20 min | 800-1200 | 9-12 | 5-6 | Chapter-style, subplots |

## Age 0-2: Lullaby Stories

**Goal**: Soothe through rhythm and sound, not narrative.

### Format
- **Duration**: 2-4 minutes
- **Words**: 50-150 total
- **Structure**: 1-3 very short "paragraphs" (more like verses)
- **Sentences**: 1-2 per section, max 5-8 words each

### Content Guidelines

**DO use**:
- Onomatopoeia: `šššš` (shushing), `bum-bum` (heartbeat), `miau`, `mu-ū`
- Repetitive sounds: "Miegok, miegok, mažyli..."
- Rhyming words and lullaby-like rhythm
- Familiar objects: blanket, moon, stars, teddy bear, mama/tėtis
- Soft action words: sleeps, cuddles, dreams, hugs

**DO NOT use**:
- Plot or conflict
- New/unfamiliar vocabulary
- Questions (cognitive stimulation)
- More than 2-3 characters

### Example Structure
```
[Section 1 - Setting the scene]
Mėnulis šviečia. Žvaigždės mirksi.
[soft sounds, 10-15 words]

...

[Section 2 - Comfort]
Meškiukas miega. Zuikutis miega.
[repetition pattern, 10-15 words]

...

[Section 3 - Sleep]
[yawns] Miegok, mažyli. [whispers] Labanakt.
[closing lullaby, 10-15 words]
```

### Audio Tags
- Heavy use of `[softly]` and `[whispers]`
- Use `[yawns]` liberally throughout (not just at end)
- Slower pace: ideal for this age

### Research Notes
> "Stories for 1-year-old babies should be very short (2-4 minutes), with simple sentences, repetitions, and everyday situations." — [Oh My Tales](https://www.ohmytales.com/a/1-to-2-years/)

> "Young children often enjoy books, songs and stories that have good rhyme, rhythm and repetition." — [Raising Children Network](https://raisingchildren.net.au/babies/play-learning/literacy-reading-stories/reading-storytelling)

## Age 2-4: First Stories

**Goal**: Introduce simple narrative with comforting predictability.

### Format
- **Duration**: 4-6 minutes
- **Words**: 150-300 total
- **Structure**: 3-5 short paragraphs
- **Sentences**: 2-3 per paragraph, max 8-12 words each

### Content Guidelines

**Themes that work**:
- Daily routines (bath time, eating, playing, sleeping)
- Family and home
- Friendly animals doing familiar things
- Simple emotions: happy, sleepy, cozy

**Story structure**:
1. **Who**: Introduce ONE main character (animal works best)
2. **What**: One simple situation (getting ready for bed, finding a cozy spot)
3. **Resolution**: Character falls asleep peacefully

**DO use**:
- Repetitive phrases: "Ir tada... ir tada..."
- Counting patterns: "Vienas meškiukas, du meškiukai..."
- Questions followed by answers: "Kur yra zuikutis? Štai jis!"
- Familiar settings: bedroom, garden, forest edge

**DO NOT use**:
- Conflict or problems to solve
- Scary elements (even "friendly" monsters)
- More than 2-3 characters
- Abstract concepts

### Example Structure
```
[Para 1 - Introduction]
[softly] Mažas ežiukas gyveno miške.
Jis turėjo minkštą guolelį.
[20-40 words, introduce character and setting]

...

[Para 2 - Simple action]
Ežiukas žiūrėjo į žvaigždes.
Žvaigždės mirksėjo: "Labanakt, ežiuk!"
[30-50 words, gentle activity]

...

[Para 3 - Getting sleepy]
[yawns] Ežiukas užmerkė akutes.
Jo guolelis buvo šiltas ir jaukus.
[30-50 words, settling down]

...

[Para 4 - Sleep]
[whispers] Visas miškas tyliai miegojo.
Labanakt, mažasis ežiuk. Labanakt.
[20-40 words, peaceful ending]
```

### Audio Tags
- Start with `[softly]`
- Use `[warmly]` for comfort moments
- Gradual transition to `[whispers]` and `[yawns]`

### Research Notes
> "At 2 years old, children better understand small adventures and begin to anticipate what comes next." — [Oh My Tales](https://www.ohmytales.com/a/1-to-2-years/)

> "The ritual of reading bedtime stories creates a sense of security and predictability, critical elements in a child's life." — [Beddy.ai](https://beddy.ai/learn/bedtime-stories-improve-sleep)

## Age 4-6: Adventure Begins

**Goal**: Gentle adventures with emotional connection and peaceful resolution.

### Format
- **Duration**: 6-10 minutes
- **Words**: 300-500 total
- **Structure**: 5-7 paragraphs (matches our 7-image format perfectly)
- **Sentences**: 3-4 per paragraph, 10-15 words each

### Content Guidelines

**Themes that work**:
- Friendship and helping others
- Discovering something wonderful
- Small adventures with safe outcomes
- Nature exploration
- Dreams and imagination

**Story structure** (7 paragraphs):
1. **Introduction**: Who and where (cozy setting)
2. **Situation**: Something interesting happens
3. **Journey begins**: Character explores
4. **Discovery**: Something wonderful found
5. **Gentle climax**: A magical moment (NOT conflict)
6. **Return**: Heading home
7. **Peace**: Safe, warm, ready for sleep

**DO use**:
- Descriptive language (but simple): "sidabrinis mėnulis", "minkštas debesis"
- Emotions: happy, curious, excited, peaceful, loved
- Dialogue between characters
- Sensory details: soft, warm, sparkly, quiet

**DO NOT use**:
- Real conflict or danger
- Villains or antagonists
- Suspense or cliffhangers
- Sad endings

### Example Structure
```
[Para 1 - Introduction]
[softly] Mažoji pelytė Milda gyveno jaukiame namelyje po senu ąžuolu.
Kiekvieną vakarą ji mėgdavo žiūrėti į žvaigždes pro langą.
[40-60 words]

...

[Para 2 - Something happens]
Vieną vakarą Milda pamatė ypač ryškią žvaigždę.
Ji švietė sidabriniu spindesiu ir tarsi kvietė.
"Ką tu man nori pasakyti?" - paklausė Milda.
[50-70 words]

...

[Para 3 - Journey begins]
[excited] Milda išėjo į lauką pažiūrėti arčiau.
Žvaigždė nušvietė takelį per pievą.
[50-70 words]

...

[Para 4 - Discovery]
[warmly] Ten, kur baigėsi takas, augo stebuklingas sodas.
Gėlės švytėjo švelnia šviesa, o drugeliai snaudė ant žiedlapių.
[50-70 words]

...

[Para 5 - Magical moment]
[whispers] Vienas mažas drugelis praleido į Mildos delną.
"Aš esu Sapnų drugelis," - sušnibždėjo jis.
[50-70 words]

...

[Para 6 - Return home]
[softly] Drugelis palydėjo Mildą namo.
Ji jautėsi laiminga ir saugi.
[40-60 words]

...

[Para 7 - Sleep]
[yawns] Milda įsitaisė savo lovelėje.
[whispers] Sapnų drugelis tyliai saugojo jos sapnus.
Labanakt, mažoji Milda. Labanakt.
[40-50 words]
```

### Audio Tags Distribution
- Para 1: `[softly]`
- Para 2-3: `[warmly]`, `[excited]`
- Para 4-5: `[whispers]`, `[playfully]`
- Para 6-7: `[softly]`, `[yawns]`, `[whispers]`

### Research Notes
> "Children from about four to six years old are just beginning to learn how to read, so parents can introduce stories with slightly more challenging vocabulary, more intricate plot lines, and characters the child will identify with." — [Story Spark](https://storyspark.ai/learn/parents-handbook-bedtime-stories)

> "Preschoolers respond well to rhythm, rhyme, and repetition, which influence how soothing a bedtime story feels." — [Charlotte Glaze](https://charlotteglaze.com/family-story-time/how-long-should-storytime-last-age-by-age-guide/)

## Age 6-8: Rich Narratives

**Goal**: Engaging stories with moral lessons and emotional depth.

### Format
- **Duration**: 10-15 minutes
- **Words**: 500-800 total
- **Structure**: 7-9 paragraphs
- **Sentences**: 4-5 per paragraph, 12-18 words each

### Content Guidelines

**Themes that work**:
- Courage and facing small fears
- Friendship and loyalty
- Problem-solving (non-violent)
- Fantasy and magic worlds
- Learning from mistakes
- Kindness and helping others

**Story structure** (7+ paragraphs):
1. **Introduction**: Character with a wish or wonder
2. **Inciting moment**: Something changes
3. **Decision**: Character chooses to act
4. **Journey/Challenge**: Faces a gentle obstacle
5. **Help arrives**: Friend or magical helper
6. **Resolution**: Problem solved through kindness/cleverness
7. **Reflection**: Character learns something
8. **(Optional) Return home**: Safe and cozy
9. **Peace**: Grateful, happy, sleepy

**CAN include**:
- Mild challenges (NOT scary)
- Simple moral lessons
- Multiple characters (3-4 max)
- Dialogue and personality
- Descriptive world-building

**STILL avoid**:
- True danger or violence
- Mean characters (make them confused/lost instead)
- Unresolved problems
- Scary imagery

### Vocabulary Enhancement
At this age, introduce 2-3 new words per story:
- Rich descriptive words: "žvilgantis" (gleaming), "slaptas" (secret)
- Emotion words: "dėkingas" (grateful), "smalsu" (curious)
- Natural in context, not forced

### Audio Tags Distribution
More varied emotional expression:
- `[excited]` for discoveries
- `[playfully]` for dialogue
- `[warmly]` for friendship moments
- `[giggles]` for light humor
- Progressive calming toward end

### Research Notes
> "Elementary-aged children (5-8 years) can engage with 15-20 minute longer narratives." — [Charlotte Glaze](https://charlotteglaze.com/family-story-time/how-long-should-storytime-last-age-by-age-guide/)

> "Reading aloud to children builds vocabulary and essential literacy skills. Listening to someone read—especially more advanced books—allows your child to absorb complex words and sentences." — [Scholastic](https://www.scholastic.com/parents/family-life/parent-child/importance-reading-bedtime-stories-to-big-kids.html)

## Age 8-9: Chapter-Style Stories

**Goal**: Sophisticated narratives that respect their growing intelligence.

### Format
- **Duration**: 15-20 minutes
- **Words**: 800-1200 total
- **Structure**: 9-12 paragraphs (or 2-3 "mini-chapters")
- **Sentences**: 5-6 per paragraph, varied lengths (8-20 words)

### Content Guidelines

**Themes that work**:
- Adventure with meaningful stakes
- Friendship trials and reconciliation
- Mystery and discovery
- Fantasy quests
- Real-world issues (gentle treatment)
- Personal growth

**Story structure** (chapter-style):

**Chapter 1** (3-4 para):
- World and character establishment
- Character has a meaningful goal
- Inciting incident

**Chapter 2** (4-5 para):
- Journey and challenges
- Meets allies/helpers
- Key discovery or skill learned

**Chapter 3** (3-4 para):
- Climax (can have real tension, but safe resolution)
- Achievement and growth
- Peaceful, reflective ending

**CAN include**:
- Real obstacles (age-appropriate)
- Character flaws and growth
- Subplot elements
- Humor and wit
- Light suspense

**Important for bedtime**:
- Even with complex plots, ending must be peaceful
- Avoid cliffhangers (it's not a series!)
- Last 2 paragraphs should always wind down

### Protagonist Age
The main character should be 9-10 years old (slightly older than reader). Children prefer to "read up" to characters slightly older than themselves.

### Audio Tags
Full range of expression:
- Use all tags throughout
- More dramatic delivery possible
- Still end with calming sequence

### Research Notes
> "Children who are seven and older need to be challenged a little more to keep them interested, and chapter books or 'big kid books' will definitely do the job." — [Story Spark](https://storyspark.ai/learn/parents-handbook-bedtime-stories)

> "Chapter books allow you to discuss some adult concepts, but from the point of view of the child. You are able to introduce some complex vocabulary, but still focus on the innocence of the characters." — [Mary Kole Editorial](https://www.marykole.com/childrens-book-age-groups)

## Universal Sleep-Inducing Principles

These apply to ALL age groups:

### 1. Pacing
- **Start**: Normal energy, engaging
- **Middle**: Gradually slow down
- **End**: Very slow, repetitive, whispered

### 2. Settings That Soothe
- Cozy beds, nests, burrows
- Moonlit nights
- Soft blankets and warm places
- Gardens and quiet forests
- Starry skies

### 3. Calming Vocabulary
Use throughout, especially at end:
- minkštas (soft)
- šiltas (warm)
- tylus (quiet)
- ramus (calm)
- jaukus (cozy)
- saugus (safe)

### 4. Avoid Before Sleep
- Problem-solving questions
- Exciting discoveries in final paragraph
- Any unresolved tension
- Loud or sudden sounds in audio
- Stimulating colors in images (use cool tones for final images)

### 5. The Golden Ending Formula
Always end stories with:
```
[yawns] [brief settling action]
[whispers] [character safe and sleepy]
Labanakt. [final word]
```

### Research Notes
> "Sleep Stories are narrative, but instead of building up tension and stakes, the idea is to unwind." — [Calm Blog](https://www.calm.com/blog/the-thinking-behind-sleep-stories)

> "Gentle, slow-paced stories can induce a state of relaxation and drowsiness, guiding the child to a peaceful sleep." — [Beddy.ai](https://beddy.ai/learn/bedtime-stories-improve-sleep)

## Image Guidelines by Age

### 0-2 years
- **Style**: Very simple, high contrast
- **Colors**: Soft pastels, avoid bright stimulating colors
- **Characters**: 1-2 per image, large and centered
- **Background**: Minimal, mostly negative space
- **Night scenes**: Dominant for sleep association

### 2-4 years
- **Style**: Soft, rounded shapes (like board book illustrations)
- **Colors**: Warm, muted palette
- **Characters**: Cute, expressive faces
- **Background**: Simple but recognizable (house, bed, forest)
- **Night elements**: Moon, stars, cozy interiors

### 4-6 years
- **Style**: Storybook illustration (our "watercolor" or "storybook" styles)
- **Colors**: Rich but not overstimulating
- **Characters**: Can show more detail and personality
- **Background**: More developed scenes
- **Magic elements**: Sparkles, soft glows, dreamy effects

### 6-9 years
- **Style**: Any of our styles work well
- **Colors**: Full palette, cool tones for endings
- **Characters**: Can show action and emotion
- **Background**: Detailed world-building
- **Final image**: Always peaceful, sleepy scene
